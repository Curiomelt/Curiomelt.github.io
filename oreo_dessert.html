How to Make Delicious Milk Caramel Oreo Dessert

Aug 28 2023 Time to make: 35 minutes Author: Ulurt Nileman

Ingredients:

  • 2 packages of Oreos (about 30 cookies)
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Whipped cream and additional crushed Oreos for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Start by crushing the Oreos. You can use a food processor or simply place the cookies in a zip-top bag and crush them with a rolling pin until finely ground.
  2. In a bowl, combine the crushed Oreos and melted butter until the mixture resembles wet sand.
  3. Press the Oreo mixture firmly into the bottom and up the sides of a 9-inch pie dish to form the crust. Place the crust in the refrigerator to set while you prepare the filling.
  4. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk and a pinch of salt until well combined.
  5. In a separate mixing bowl, whip the heavy whipping cream and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.
  6. Gently fold the whipped cream into the milk caramel mixture until smooth and creamy.
  7. Retrieve the Oreo crust from the refrigerator and pour the milk caramel filling into the crust, spreading it evenly.
  8. Place the Milk Caramel Oreo Pie in the refrigerator and let it chill for at least 4 hours, or until set.
  9. Before serving, garnish the pie with dollops of whipped cream and a generous sprinkling of crushed Oreos.

Optional Variations:

  • For an extra touch of indulgence, consider drizzling some warm caramel sauce over the whipped cream topping.
  • You can also add chopped nuts, such as toasted pecans or almonds, to the caramel filling for added crunch and flavor.

Tips for Success:

  • Make sure the whipped cream is properly whipped to stiff peaks before folding it into the milk caramel mixture. This ensures a creamy and light texture.
  • When pressing the Oreo crust into the pie dish, use the back of a spoon or a flat-bottomed glass to create an even and compact layer.
  • For a no-bake version of this dessert, you can chill the pie in the freezer for a few hours until set.
