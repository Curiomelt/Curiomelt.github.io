Some of The Best Netflix Documentaries Ever Made

Apr 19th 2023 4 Min read Author: Ethan Davis

If you're a documentary lover, Netflix is a treasure trove of thought-provoking, eye-opening, and emotionally gripping films. With a wide range of topics, from true crime to nature, politics to art, there's something for everyone. To help you navigate the vast selection, we've curated a list of the top 10 best documentaries on Netflix that are a must-watch for any documentary enthusiast.

1. "Blackfish" (2013)

Look into the complex web of orca whale captivity in Gabriela Cowperthwaite's moving documentary "Blackfish," which focuses on the harrowing story of Tilikum, a captive killer whale whose sinister path intertwined with the deaths of several trainers. This masterpiece raises important issues and prompts reflection on the moral dilemmas involved in keeping these magnificent animals in captivity for our enjoyment. "Blackfish" emerges as a siren call, imploring society to nurture a greater sense of awareness and devotion for the protection of marine life while exposing the immense cost that confinement on these magnificent organisms, as the seas boil with emotions.

2. "13th" (2016)

The brilliant Ava DuVernay's "13th" serves as a direct controversial of the complex system linking racism, justice, and the shocking depths of widespread jailing in the United States. This work of art examines the 13th Amendment to the United States Constitution, a seemingly empowering declaration but regrettably had a fatal defect that continued the plague of imprisonment faced by Black Americans. A cruel focus puts light on the deep racism that exists inside the criminal justice system as "13th" carefully reveals its dark core and reveals its destructive effects on communities of colour. Through the decades, there has been an alarm call that generated a passionate urge to tackle racism's and work to transform the very foundation of the criminal justice system.

3. "Won't You Be My Neighbor?" (2018)

Morgan Neville lovingly creates a soul-stirring ode to the life and long impact of Fred Rogers, the beloved presenter of the renowned children's television show "Mister Rogers' Neighbourhood." "Won't You Be My Neighbour?" creates a touching image of Rogers' unshakable dedication to compassion, empathy, and social change through historical video mixed with moving interviews. This work tugs at the heartstrings of compassion and serves as a stark reminder of the huge impact that one individual, motivated by a firm conviction in the value of fostering emotional well-being, may have on generations of impressionable minds— both young and old.

4. "The Social Dilemma" (2020)

Explore the unpredictable world of social networking and gaze into the depths of its tempting charm with Jeff Orlowski's thought-provoking documentary, "The Social Dilemma." As the curtain of illusion is gradually removed, former insiders in the IT sector offer insight on the deceptive methods used to trap people inside the web of social media platforms, exposing the negative effects on mental health, privacy, and even the foundations of democracy. "The Social Dilemma" urges society to address the inherent risks present in our linked digital environment. It compels viewers to reevaluate their online habits and reflect on the widespread use of technology in our daily lives.

5. "Jiro Dreams of Sushi" (2011)

The captivating world of culinary expertise "Jiro Dreams of Sushi," by David Gelb's is visually beautiful epic. The documentary reveals the life and strong passion of Jiro Ono, an 80-year-old sushi maestri located in the heart of Tokyo. This documentary provides a close-up look at Jiro's unwavering quest for culinary perfection and illustrates the complex dance that a father and his kids engage in as they work to push the limits of their abilities. With each mouthwatering bite, "Jiro Dreams of Sushi" transforms into a fascinating journey that delves into the enthralling depths of Japanese cuisine and the never-ending pursuit of exceptional perfection.

6. "Free Solo" (2018)

Get ready to be mesmerised by the magnificent achievements of defying gravity as Jimmy Chin and Elizabeth Chai Vasarhelyi recount the amazing journey of rock climber Alex Honnold in "Free Solo." As Honnold faces his innermost fears and tests the limits of human ability while free solo climbing, this high-stakes cinematic masterpiece unfolds against the majestic background of Yosemite National Park's El Capitan. The passionate mental rollercoaster produced by the nausea-inducing cinematography coupled with Honnold's daring achievement has viewers seated on the edge of mortality yet fascinated by the intriguing tale at hand.

7. "The Keepers" (2017)

"The Keepers," a thrilling true crime documentary series from Ryan White, explores the mysterious and unsolved death of Sister Cathy Cesnik, an acclaimed nun and educator in Baltimore, Maryland. Former students and investigators set out on a perilous trip through the Catholic Church's murky depths of institutional abuse, corruption, and cover-ups in an effort to uncover the truth hidden behind a thick layer of deception. "The Keepers" emerges in this chilling story as a compelling endorsement, giving light on a tragedy that lingers as a sharp reminder of the unanswered questions that persist in our society.

8. "Chasing Coral" (2017)

Experience a visually stunning and time-sensitive journey as Jeff Orlowski's "Chasing Coral" sets out on a mission to highlight the disastrous effects of climate change on coral reefs throughout the world. In this tragic film, a group of scientists, photographers, and divers capture the terrifying bleaching occurrences brought on by rising ocean temperatures. "Chasing Coral" reveals the growing risk that hangs over our seas through an arrangement of striking visuals, acting as an urgent call to action to safeguard these fragile ecosystems from destruction. This work serves as a passionate plea, bringing to light the worldwide catastrophe of climate change and its catastrophic impacts for the beauty of our planet.

Conclusion

Documentaries are a powerful medium that can inform, inspire, and provoke thought. The documentaries highlighted in this article, available on Netflix, cover a wide range of topics and genres, offering viewers a diverse array of captivating storytelling, stunning visuals, and thought-provoking insights. These documentaries provide a unique perspective on the world we live in, highlighting the complexities and challenges faced by different communities, individuals, and environments. They raise important questions, challenge assumptions, and encourage viewers to think critically about the world around them. Documentaries also have the power to inspire action and change, igniting discussions and creating awareness.
